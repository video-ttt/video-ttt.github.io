Test-Time Training on Video Streams

Renhao Wang 1*, Yu Sun 1*, Yossi Gandelsman 1, Xinlei Chen 2, Alexei A. Efros 1, Xiaolong Wang 3
1UC Berkeley, 2Meta AI, 3UC San Diego
*Equal Contribution
arXiv Dataset, Code & Models

Abstract

Traditionally, single-image models are evaluated on videos as collections of unordered frames. Instead, we evaluate on videos in temporal order, making a prediction on the current frame before the next arrives, after training at test time on frames from the recent past. We investigate techniques for test-time training without ground truth labels, and find that masked autoencoders perform the best. While most of the components of our algorithm have been introduced before, our novelty lies in combining them effectively on videos. We significantly improve the state-of-the-art on real-world applications, and discover that forgetting can be beneficial in our streaming setting.
